## What This Means For Us

**The ZKP age verification space is heating up fast.** Google's `longfellow-zk` is the clearest validation that Signet's problem space matters, but it's also the strongest competitive signal. Google has distribution (Wallet integration) and brand trust. Signet's differentiator is decentralisation — no Google account required, no central authority, professional bodies as trust anchors. This positioning needs to be crystal clear in documentation and developer outreach.

**AI agent identity on Nostr is an emerging design space with no winner yet.** NIP-XX AI Agent Messages, NIP-AC DVM Agent Coordination, and pablof7z's AI SDK infrastructure sprint all point to the same conclusion: AI agents will need identity and trust on Nostr. Signet's credential system could verify that an AI agent is operated by a verified entity (Tier 3/4). This is a greenfield opportunity — nobody has shipped agent identity verification on Nostr yet.

**The EU regulatory timeline is now a forcing function.** EUDI Wallet mandate at end of 2026, interim age verification piloting in 5 countries, Animo shipping wallet infrastructure. Signet doesn't need to be the EUDI wallet — but it needs to be interoperable with VC standards coming out of W3C (Data Model v2.1, July 2026) and DIF. The Nostr DID Method is the bridge: if Signet credentials can be expressed as W3C VCs over Nostr DIDs, we participate in the EU ecosystem without abandoning decentralisation.

**papiche's NIP-101 and fiatjaf's pyramid are the closest Nostr-native competitors.** Both are building identity/trust layers on Nostr — NIP-101 with peer-validated credentials (overlaps Signet Tier 2 vouching) and pyramid with social proof ladders. Neither has Signet's professional verification tiers or ZKP layer, but they have active builders and could capture the "good enough" identity slot if Signet doesn't ship first.
